How many move? The 20% rule, made exact

"Top/bottom 20%" has to become an integer count that behaves for a cohort of 3 or 300. The open choice is how to round; the edges and tie-breaks are shown as settled.

Why floor: 2·floor(0.2N) is always < N, so there's always a non-empty "stay" middle and nobody can be both promoted and demoted — no special small-cohort guard needed. Cohorts of ≤4 simply don't move (too small to compete).

Settled: edges & tie-breaks

Bronze is the floor → its bottom-20% stay (no demotion below Bronze).

Diamond is the summit → its top-20% stay (no promotion above Diamond).

Ties (equal weekly XP at a boundary) are broken by a stable sort on LearnerId — already how the cohort query orders, so settlement is deterministic.

How should we round "20% of N" to a count?

A

floor(0.2 × N) ★

Always leaves a middle, never overlaps, ≤4-person cohorts rest. Simplest with the strongest guarantees.

B

round(0.2 × N)

A bit more movement (e.g. N=8 → 2 instead of 1); starts moving at N=3. Still overlap-safe at these sizes.

C

ceil(0.2 × N)

Most aggressive (N=12 → 3 each). Can fully reshuffle tiny cohorts (e.g. N=2 → everyone moves).
