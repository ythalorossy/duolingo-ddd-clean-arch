Q2 · When does a "day" end? (the timezone trap)

"Consecutive days" is meaningless until we define a day boundary. Here's a learner in New York (UTC−05) who studies late at night.

The same two lessons, interpreted two ways
Lesson 1: Mon 11:30 PM local  =  Tue 04:30 UTC
Lesson 2: Wed 7:00 AM local  =  Wed 12:00 UTC

InterpretationL1 dayL2 dayGap?Streak result
Local date (New York)MonWedTue missed✅ resets — correct (they really did skip Tuesday)
UTC dateTueWednone❌ keeps streak — WRONG (Tue→Wed looks consecutive)

UTC boundaries silently award streaks the learner didn't earn (and steal ones they did). Correct streaks require the learner's local day boundary.

Decision: which day boundary?

A

Learner's local time zone (recommended)

Convert each LessonCompleted's UTC instant to the learner's local date. Correct, but we must know their time zone.

B

UTC for everyone

Trivial to implement, no time zone needed — but produces wrong streaks for anyone not near UTC. Not acceptable for a real product.
