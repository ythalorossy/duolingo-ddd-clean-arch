Q4 · Streak freeze — in this slice, or the next?

A streak freeze is a consumable that bridges a missed day so the streak survives.

What a freeze does (missed Thursday, 1 freeze available)
Mon Tue Wed Thu Fri
 ✅  ✅  ✅  ❄️  ✅
 1   2   3   3   4     ← freeze bridges Thu; streak preserved (Thu doesn't add)
Without a freeze, Thursday's gap would reset the streak to 1 on Friday.

What "freeze" actually drags in

  • Inventory — how many freezes a learner holds, and a max (Duolingo caps at 2)
  • Earning / buying — freezes come from XP, gems, or purchase (ties into monetization — out of scope)
  • Auto-apply rules — consumed automatically on a miss; how many days can be bridged
  • Consumption timing — applied lazily at the next qualifying day (fits our derived model)

Where it plugs into our model (the extension point)

Our transition's "gap" case (D > lastDate + 1) is exactly where freeze slots in:
compute missedDays = D − lastDate − 1; if availableFreezes ≥ missedDays, consume them and continue; else reset. The core model already has the seam.

Decision

A

Defer freeze to the next slice (recommended)

This slice = core streak (derived, timezone, longest). Keep the gap-handling seam ready. Freeze becomes its own focused sub-project (inventory, earning, auto-apply).

B

Include a minimal freeze now

Inventory + auto-consume on a 1-day gap, freezes granted via a command (earning/buying still deferred). Roughly doubles the slice.
